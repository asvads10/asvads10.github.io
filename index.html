// my journey

How I Got Here

🌱

2019 — The Problem That Started It All

A mountain of unstructured legal documents. One question nobody had answered — "Can a machine read this the way a lawyer does?" No LLMs. No magic. Just Python, XGBoost, TF-IDF, and a refusal to give up. I built document classifiers from scratch using classical ML — and got completely hooked.

📈

2019–2022 — Three Years in the Trenches

Three years going deeper — BERT, Azure Form Recognizer, AWS SageMaker, serverless architectures, NLP pipelines. Built legal extraction systems, intelligent specification reviewers, and document classification tools. Every project messier than the last. Every solution sharper. Skills forged under real pressure with real deadlines — no sandbox, no shortcuts.

✈️

2022–2024 — Betting on Myself

After three years of building production AI, I made a bold move. Moved to the US and enrolled in the Masters in Computer Science at the University of North Texas. Not because I had to. Because I wanted to understand what I was building at a level most engineers never reach — theory meeting practice in real time.

🔬

2023 — GenAI Arrived. I Was Already Ready.

While still finishing my Masters, GPT-4, LangChain, RAG, and Agentic AI exploded onto the scene. The problems I had spent years solving the hard way suddenly had a powerful new language — and my classical ML foundation made me immediately fluent. I didn't wait to graduate. Landed a GenAI Consultant role and started building production systems simultaneously.

⚡

2024–Present — Building What Matters

Masters completed. 95% accuracy on legal AI. MRI predictive maintenance. Invoice automation across geographies. Document review cut by 40% across 50,000+ files. Today I design end-to-end intelligent systems using GPT-4o, Claude, Gemini, LangGraph, and Agentic AI — systems that don't just predict. They reason, retrieve, and act.

"I didn't chase the hype. I built the foundation first. Classical ML taught me how AI thinks. A Masters taught me why it works. GenAI showed me how far it can go."
